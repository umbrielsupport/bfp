Frequently asked questions

What is unified communications as a service?

Unified Communications as a Service (UCaaS) is a cloud-based solution that combines various communication methods within a business into a single, unified platform. This service typically includes voice calls, video conferencing, instant messaging, and email, all accessible from one interface. By integrating these different modes of communication, UCaaS simplifies and streamlines business interactions, enhancing collaboration and productivity. The cloud-based nature of the service ensures flexibility, scalability, and the benefit of continuous updates and improvements without the need for significant equipment investments.

What are managed VoIP services?

Managed VoIP services are a comprehensive set of solutions where a VoIP supplier, like ourselves, handle all aspects of your business's voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) communication. This includes the setup, management, and maintenance of your VoIP system. The advantage of a fully managed service is that it removes the burden of self managing your own phone systems, and instead, our team takes care of the technical details, from system updates to troubleshooting, allowing you to focus on your business without worrying about your communication infrastructure.

Can we replace our business landlines with VoIP?

Absolutely, you can replace your business landlines with VoIP. VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) offers a more flexible and often more cost-effective solution for business communication. It allows you to make and receive calls over the internet, offering additional features like voicemail, call forwarding, and video conferencing that traditional landlines don't typically provide. Transitioning to VoIP can be seamless, and we can assist in porting your existing phone numbers, ensuring there's no disruption to your business operations.

What are the disadvantages of using VoIP?

While VoIP offers many advantages, there are a handful of disadvantages to be aware of:

  • Dependence on Internet Connection: VoIP relies on your internet connection. If your internet service is down or unstable, your VoIP service will be affected, potentially leading to dropped calls or poor call quality. This issue can be overcome with the right connectivity and failover network infrastructure.
  • Power Outages: Unlike traditional landlines that operate during a power outage, VoIP requires power for the internet and VoIP hardware to function. Planning ahead for this, it's possible to install a battery backup which ensures phones remain operational, as well as installing softphone applications which can be used with mobile phones on cellular networks.
  • Voice Quality and Latency Issues: Although rare with good internet connections, VoIP can sometimes experience latency, jitter, or packet loss, which can affect voice quality. This issue can be overcome with dedicated voice internet connections or leased lines which are segregated to provide a dedicated amount of bandwidth to the phone network at all times.
  • Security Concerns: VoIP systems, like any internet-based technology, can be vulnerable to cyber threats. It's essential to have robust security measures in place, like the advanced tools we provide as part of our cyber security service.

By understanding the potential disadvantages and how to effectively address each issue, it's possible to design and maintain a highly reliable and secure communication system.

Are calls over VoIP free?

Calls over VoIP are not universally free, but they can be significantly cheaper than traditional phone lines, especially for long-distance and international calls. Calls made from one VoIP system to another VoIP system, often within the same service provider, are usually free. However, when you call a traditional landline or a mobile phone from a VoIP system, there may be charges involved. These charges are typically lower than traditional phone service rates. Additionally, many VoIP service providers, including ourselves, offer various plans with inclusive minutes or low-cost options for business users, which can lead to substantial cost savings for your business.

What happens if internet goes down with VoIP?

With us, your VoIP system is hosted in the cloud which means it's always online even if your office internet goes offline. In this situation, there are a few ways to still receive calls inside your office even with no internet:

  • Cellular Data: Using the mobile apps we provide, you can switch to a cellular data network to make and receive calls when your primary internet connection is down.
  • Redundant Internet Connection: Most businesses have a backup internet connection to ensure continuous VoIP service even if one connection fails. Often it's advised to have a dedicated VoIP internet connection, and a failover connection that utilises a segment of your regular internet service.
  • Call Forwarding: In an absolute worse case scenario, VoIP systems allow you to set up call forwarding to another phone, such as a mobile device. This way, calls can still be received even if your primary internet connection is down.

In general, there isn't much of a concern if internet goes down as long as team members have access to the mobile apps. Proactively creating strategies that account for scenarios just like this is how we're able to help you to create the ultimate communication system.
